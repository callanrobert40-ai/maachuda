Classic Tuscan Lasagna

Prep: 30 min • Cook: 1 hr 30 min • Serves: 8 • 520 kcal per serving

This is the lasagna that ends the search for all other lasagna recipes. Three distinct layers of flavor: a slow-simmered Bolognese ragù made with a soffritto of onion, carrot, and celery; a velvety béchamel sauce with a whisper of nutmeg; and stretchy, golden mozzarella with sharp Parmesan on top. It takes a little effort, but the result — those perfect layered slices with crispy edges — is one of the most rewarding things you can bake.

Ingredients

Bolognese Ragù

  • 500g ground beef (or 250g beef + 250g pork)
  • 1 large onion, finely diced
  • 2 medium carrots, finely diced
  • 2 stalks celery, finely diced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 can (400g) crushed tomatoes
  • 2 tablespoons tomato paste
  • ½ cup dry red wine
  • 1 cup beef broth
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon dried basil
  • 1 bay leaf, salt, and pepper

Béchamel Sauce

  • 60g butter (4 tablespoons)
  • 60g all-purpose flour (4 tablespoons)
  • 750ml whole milk
  • Pinch of ground nutmeg
  • Salt and white pepper to taste

Assembly

  • 12 lasagna sheets (dried or fresh)
  • 250g mozzarella, shredded
  • 50g Parmesan, freshly grated
  • Fresh basil leaves for garnish

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Make the ragù: Heat olive oil in a large, heavy pot over medium-high heat. Add ground beef and cook, breaking it apart, until deeply browned — about 8 minutes. Remove meat and set aside. In the same pot, add the soffritto (onion, carrot, celery) and cook for 8 minutes until softened. Add garlic (1 min). Return the browned meat. Deglaze with red wine, scraping up the fond. Add crushed tomatoes, tomato paste, beef broth, oregano, basil, and bay leaf. Bring to a simmer, then reduce heat to low. Cook uncovered for 45 minutes, stirring occasionally. Season with salt and pepper. Remove bay leaf.
  2. Make the béchamel: In a medium saucepan, melt butter over medium heat. Add flour and whisk constantly for 2 minutes until the roux turns pale golden (this cooks out the raw flour taste). Gradually pour in milk, whisking continuously to prevent lumps. Cook for 6-8 minutes, whisking often, until the sauce thickens enough to coat the back of a spoon. Remove from heat. Season with nutmeg, salt, and white pepper.
  3. Prepare the pasta: If using dried lasagna sheets, cook them in salted boiling water for 2 minutes less than the package recommends (they'll finish cooking in the oven). Drain and lay flat on a lightly oiled surface so they don't stick. If using fresh sheets or no-boil noodles, skip this step.
  4. Assemble the lasagna: Preheat oven to 180°C (350°F). Spread a thin layer of ragù across the bottom of a 9x13-inch (23x33 cm) baking dish. Layer as follows: pasta sheets → ragù → béchamel → sprinkle of mozzarella. Repeat this layering 3-4 times. Finish the top layer with béchamel, remaining mozzarella, and all the grated Parmesan.
  5. Bake: Cover the dish with aluminum foil (tented, not touching the cheese). Bake for 25 minutes. Remove the foil and bake for another 15-20 minutes until the top is golden, bubbling, and slightly crispy at the edges.
  6. Rest and serve: Remove from the oven and let the lasagna rest for 15 minutes before cutting — this is essential for clean slices. Garnish with fresh basil leaves and serve.

Tips & Variations

  • Make ahead: Assemble the lasagna up to 24 hours before baking. Cover with plastic wrap and refrigerate. Add 10 minutes to the covered baking time if baking from cold.
  • Freeze it: Baked or unbaked lasagna freezes well for up to 3 months. Thaw overnight in the fridge before reheating at 180°C for 30-40 minutes.
  • Vegetarian version: Replace the beef with sautéed mushrooms, zucchini, and spinach for a hearty vegetable lasagna.
  • The resting step matters: Cutting too soon gives you a soupy mess. Those 15 minutes allow the layers to set and the cheese to firm up slightly.

Nutrition Information (per serving)
