Spicy Chana Masala

Prep: 10 min • Cook: 25 min • Serves: 4 • 320 kcal per serving

Chana Masala (Chole) is North India's most popular street food — spicy, tangy, and incredibly satisfying. This recipe produces a thick, flavorful gravy bursting with whole spices and plump chickpeas. It's naturally vegan, packed with 15 grams of protein per serving, and comes together in just 30 minutes. The secret to restaurant-quality chana masala is cooking the onions until deeply golden and using amchur (dry mango powder) for that signature tangy kick.

Ingredients

  • 2 cans (800g total) chickpeas, drained and rinsed
  • 2 large onions, finely diced
  • 3 medium tomatoes, pureed (or 1 can crushed tomatoes)
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 2 green chilies, slit lengthwise
  • 2 tablespoons neutral oil
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 2 whole cloves
  • 1 small cinnamon stick
  • 2 teaspoons ground coriander
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon Kashmiri red chili powder
  • ½ teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon amchur (dry mango powder) or juice of ½ lemon
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • Fresh coriander (cilantro) for garnish
  • Salt to taste

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Temper the whole spices: Heat oil in a heavy-bottomed pan over medium heat. Add cumin seeds, bay leaf, cloves, and cinnamon stick. Let the cumin seeds splutter and the spices become fragrant — about 30 seconds.
  2. Cook the onions: Add diced onions and cook, stirring regularly, until they turn golden brown — a full 8-10 minutes. Don't rush this step; deeply caramelized onions form the flavor foundation of the dish.
  3. Add ginger-garlic and chilies: Add ginger-garlic paste and slit green chilies. Cook for 1 minute, stirring constantly to prevent burning.
  4. Build the gravy: Pour in the tomato puree. Add coriander powder, cumin powder, chili powder, and turmeric. Cook on medium heat, stirring occasionally, until the oil starts to separate from the masala — about 8 minutes. This is the key indicator that the gravy is ready.
  5. Add chickpeas: Add drained chickpeas and ½ cup of water. Stir well to coat the chickpeas in the gravy. Bring to a simmer and cook for 10 minutes. Using the back of a wooden spoon, gently mash about a quarter of the chickpeas against the side of the pan — this thickens the gravy naturally.
  6. Finish with garam masala and amchur: Sprinkle in garam masala and amchur powder (or squeeze lemon juice). Stir well and cook for 1 more minute. Taste and adjust salt and spice levels.
  7. Serve: Remove bay leaf, cloves, and cinnamon stick. Garnish generously with fresh coriander leaves. Serve hot with steamed rice, warm roti, bhatura, or crispy puri.

Tips & Variations

  • Use dried chickpeas for best texture: Soak 1.5 cups of dried chickpeas overnight, then pressure cook with a pinch of baking soda until tender. The texture is noticeably better than canned.
  • Tea bag trick for dark color: Traditional chana masala has a dark brown color. To achieve this, boil chickpeas with a tea bag or tea leaves in a muslin cloth.
  • Store well: This curry improves overnight. Refrigerate for up to 4 days or freeze for up to 2 months.
  • Pair perfectly: Serve with sliced raw onion, lemon wedges, and pickled green chilies for an authentic North Indian experience.

Nutrition Information (per serving)

Calories: 320 kcal • Protein: 15g • Carbohydrates: 42g • Fat: 10g • Fiber: 12g • Sodium: 480mg
