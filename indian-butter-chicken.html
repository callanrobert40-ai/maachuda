Authentic Butter Chicken

Prep: 20 min + 1 hr marination • Cook: 30 min • Serves: 4 • 480 kcal per serving

Butter Chicken (Murgh Makhani) is the crown jewel of Indian cuisine — and making it at home is easier than you think. This recipe delivers the exact same velvety, mildly spiced tomato-cream sauce you get at your favorite restaurant. The secret? Marinating the chicken in spiced yogurt, charring it for smoky flavor, and finishing the sauce with cashew paste and kasuri methi (dried fenugreek leaves). This recipe has been perfected over dozens of test batches.

Ingredients

Chicken Marinade

  • 500g boneless chicken thighs, cut into chunks
  • ½ cup plain yogurt
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 teaspoon Kashmiri red chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • Juice of ½ lemon
  • Salt to taste

Makhani Sauce

  • 2 tablespoons butter + 1 tablespoon oil
  • 1 large onion, finely diced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1-inch piece fresh ginger, grated
  • 1 can (400g) crushed tomatoes
  • 10 raw cashews, soaked and blended into paste
  • ½ cup heavy cream
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1 teaspoon cumin powder
  • ½ teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 1 teaspoon kasuri methi (dried fenugreek leaves)
  • Salt to taste

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Marinate the chicken: Combine chicken pieces with yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, chili powder, turmeric, garam masala, lemon juice, and salt in a bowl. Mix well, cover, and refrigerate for at least 1 hour (overnight is ideal for maximum flavor).
  2. Char the chicken: Heat a heavy skillet or grill pan over high heat. Cook the marinated chicken pieces for 3-4 minutes per side until charred and cooked through. The charring creates a smoky depth. Set aside.
  3. Build the sauce: In the same pan (or a fresh pot), melt butter with oil over medium heat. Add diced onion and cook until deep golden, about 8-10 minutes. Add garlic and grated ginger, cook for 1 minute.
  4. Add tomatoes and spices: Pour in crushed tomatoes. Add garam masala, cumin, coriander, and sugar. Stir well and simmer for 10 minutes until the sauce thickens and the oil begins to separate from the edges.
  5. Blend and enrich: Remove from heat and blend until smooth using an immersion blender. Return to the pan. Stir in the cashew paste and heavy cream. Simmer on low heat for 5 minutes.
  6. Finish: Add the charred chicken pieces to the sauce. Simmer gently for 10 minutes. Crush the kasuri methi between your palms and sprinkle over the curry. Stir once and remove from heat.
  7. Serve: Garnish with a swirl of cream and a pat of butter. Serve hot with steamed basmati rice or warm garlic naan.

Tips & Variations

  • Lighter version: Replace heavy cream with coconut cream for a dairy-free alternative with a subtle sweetness.
  • More heat: Add 1-2 slit green chilies when sautéing the onions. Remove before blending if you want flavor without extreme heat.
  • Use chicken breast? Yes, but cut into smaller pieces and don't overcook — thighs are more forgiving and stay juicier.
  • Kasuri methi is essential: This ingredient is the secret to authentic butter chicken flavor. Find it at any Indian grocery store.
