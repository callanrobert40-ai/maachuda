Creamy Roasted Pumpkin Soup

Prep: 10 min • Cook: 30 min • Serves: 4 • 290 kcal per serving

This velvety pumpkin soup is autumn in a bowl. Roasting the pumpkin first caramelizes its natural sugars, creating a depth of flavor you simply can't get from boiling. Blended with coconut milk and warm spices, it's the kind of soup that makes you close your eyes with every spoonful. Naturally vegan, gluten-free, and incredibly easy to make.

Ingredients

  • 1 kg pumpkin (butternut or kent), peeled, seeded, and cubed
  • 1 large yellow onion, diced
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 can (400ml) full-fat coconut milk
  • 3 cups (750ml) vegetable broth
  • 2 tablespoons extra virgin olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • ½ teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • ½ teaspoon smoked paprika
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste
  • Garnish: toasted pumpkin seeds, fresh thyme sprigs, drizzle of olive oil

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Roast the pumpkin: Preheat oven to 200°C (400°F). Toss pumpkin cubes with 1 tablespoon olive oil, salt, and pepper on a lined baking sheet. Spread in a single layer and roast for 25 minutes until fork-tender and caramelized on the edges.
  2. Sauté the aromatics: While the pumpkin roasts, heat the remaining olive oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add diced onion and cook until soft and translucent, about 5 minutes. Add minced garlic, cumin, nutmeg, and smoked paprika — stir for 1 minute until fragrant.
  3. Combine and simmer: Add the roasted pumpkin, vegetable broth, and coconut milk to the pot. Stir to combine and bring to a gentle simmer. Cook for 5 minutes to meld the flavors.
  4. Blend until silky: Remove from heat. Using an immersion blender (or working in batches with a countertop blender), blend until completely smooth and velvety. Return to low heat. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  5. Serve: Ladle into warm bowls. Garnish with toasted pumpkin seeds, a sprig of fresh thyme, and a generous swirl of olive oil. Serve with crusty bread for dipping.

Tips & Variations

  • Make it ahead: This soup stores beautifully in the fridge for up to 5 days, and it actually tastes better the next day as the flavors deepen.
  • Freezer friendly: Freeze in portions for up to 3 months. Thaw overnight in the fridge and reheat gently on the stove.
  • Extra creamy: For an even richer soup, add a tablespoon of cashew butter or coconut cream when blending.
  • Swap the pumpkin: Butternut squash, sweet potato, or kabocha squash all work wonderfully here.

Nutrition Information (per serving)
