Hearty Lentil & Vegetable Stew

Prep: 10 min • Cook: 35 min • Serves: 4 • 380 kcal per serving

This is the stew you make when you want something hearty, nourishing, and deeply comforting without spending hours in the kitchen. Green lentils cook right in the pot with carrots, celery, tomatoes, and a warm blend of cumin and smoked paprika. At 24 grams of protein per serving, it's one of the most satisfying plant-based meals you'll ever make — and it costs less than a few dollars per serving.

Ingredients

  • 1½ cups green or brown lentils (300g), rinsed and picked over
  • 2 medium carrots, peeled and diced
  • 2 stalks celery, diced
  • 1 large yellow onion, diced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 can (400g) crushed tomatoes
  • 4 cups (1 liter) vegetable broth
  • 2 cups fresh spinach or kale, roughly chopped
  • 2 tablespoons extra virgin olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • ½ teaspoon ground turmeric
  • 1 bay leaf
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste
  • Juice of ½ lemon, to finish
  • Crusty bread for serving

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Sauté the vegetables: Heat olive oil in a large, heavy-bottomed pot or Dutch oven over medium heat. Add diced onion, carrots, and celery. Cook, stirring occasionally, for 5 minutes until the onion is soft and translucent.
  2. Bloom the spices: Add minced garlic, cumin, smoked paprika, and turmeric. Stir constantly for 1 minute until fragrant — be careful not to burn the garlic.
  3. Add lentils and liquid: Add rinsed lentils, crushed tomatoes, vegetable broth, and bay leaf. Stir to combine. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat to a gentle simmer.
  4. Simmer until tender: Cook uncovered for 25-30 minutes, stirring every 10 minutes to prevent sticking. The lentils should be tender but still hold their shape. If the stew becomes too thick, add a splash of broth or water.
  5. Add greens and finish: Stir in the spinach or kale and cook for 2 minutes until wilted. Remove the bay leaf. Squeeze in lemon juice and season generously with salt and pepper.
  6. Serve: Ladle into deep bowls and serve with thick slices of crusty bread for soaking up every last drop.

Tips & Variations

  • Don't skip the lemon: The squeeze of lemon at the end brightens the entire stew and balances the earthiness of the lentils.
  • Batch cook and freeze: This stew freezes beautifully for up to 3 months. It actually improves in flavor after a day.
  • Add grains: Serve over brown rice or couscous for an even heartier meal.
  • Spice it up: Add ½ teaspoon of cayenne pepper or a diced jalapeño with the garlic for heat.

Nutrition Information (per serving)

Calories: 380 kcal • Protein: 24g • Carbohydrates: 56g • Fat: 6g • Fiber: 18g • Sodium: 510mg
