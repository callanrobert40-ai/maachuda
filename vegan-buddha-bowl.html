Rainbow Buddha Bowl

Prep: 10 min • Cook: 20 min • Serves: 2 • 520 kcal per serving

This Rainbow Buddha Bowl is the kind of meal that makes you feel good inside and out. Bursting with color, packed with plant protein, and drizzled with the creamiest tahini dressing — it's proof that healthy eating is anything but boring. Each bowl delivers 22 grams of protein and is ready in just 30 minutes, making it perfect for weeknight dinners or meal prep.

Ingredients

For the Bowl

  • 1 large sweet potato, peeled and cubed (~300g)
  • 1 can (400g) chickpeas, drained and rinsed
  • 1 cup quinoa (180g)
  • 1 ripe avocado, sliced
  • 2 cups fresh baby spinach (60g)
  • 1 cup shredded purple cabbage
  • 1 medium carrot, julienned
  • 2 tbsp extra virgin olive oil
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • Sesame seeds for garnish

Tahini Dressing

  • ¼ cup tahini
  • 2 tbsp fresh lemon juice
  • 1 tbsp maple syrup
  • 2-3 tbsp warm water
  • Pinch of sea salt

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Roast the sweet potato and chickpeas: Preheat your oven to 200°C (400°F). Toss the cubed sweet potato and drained chickpeas with olive oil, smoked paprika, salt, and pepper on a lined baking sheet. Spread in a single layer. Roast for 20 minutes, flipping halfway, until the sweet potato is tender and the chickpeas are crispy and golden.
  2. Cook the quinoa: While the vegetables roast, rinse the quinoa thoroughly under cold running water (this removes the bitter coating). Place in a medium saucepan with 2 cups of water. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat to low. Cover and simmer for 15 minutes until all the water is absorbed. Remove from heat, fluff with a fork, and let it sit covered for 5 minutes.
  3. Make the tahini dressing: In a small bowl, whisk together the tahini, fresh lemon juice, maple syrup, and a pinch of sea salt. The mixture will seize up — that's normal. Gradually add warm water, one tablespoon at a time, whisking continuously until the dressing is smooth, creamy, and pourable. Taste and adjust seasoning.
  4. Assemble your bowls: Divide the fluffy quinoa between two large bowls. Arrange the roasted sweet potato, crispy chickpeas, fresh spinach leaves, shredded purple cabbage, julienned carrot, and sliced avocado in neat sections around the bowl.
  5. Dress and serve: Drizzle generously with tahini dressing. Sprinkle with sesame seeds and finish with a squeeze of fresh lemon juice. Serve immediately and enjoy!

Tips & Variations

  • Meal prep friendly: Cook quinoa and roast vegetables ahead. Store separately in the fridge for up to 4 days. Assemble fresh bowls when ready to eat.
  • Swap the grains: Brown rice, farro, or bulgur wheat all work beautifully in place of quinoa.
  • Nut-free option: Replace tahini with sunflower seed butter for a nut-free and sesame-free dressing.
  • Add extra protein: Crumble baked tofu or tempeh over the top for an additional 15g of protein per serving.
  • Seasonal swaps: In summer, use fresh corn and cherry tomatoes. In winter, try roasted beets and kale.

Nutrition Information (per serving)

Calories: 520 kcal • Protein: 22g • Carbohydrates: 68g • Fat: 18g • Fiber: 14g • Sugar: 12g • Sodium: 380mg
