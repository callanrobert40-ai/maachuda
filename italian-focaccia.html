Rosemary & Garlic Focaccia

Prep: 15 min + 1 hr rise • Bake: 25 min • Serves: 8 • 310 kcal per serving

Focaccia is the bread that makes you fall in love with baking. It's forgiving, it's fun (who doesn't love pressing dimples into dough?), and the result is always spectacular: a golden, olive-oil-soaked flatbread with crispy edges, a pillowy-soft interior, and the intoxicating aroma of fresh rosemary and garlic. This recipe uses no special equipment — just a mixing bowl, a baking sheet, and your hands. It's ready in under 2 hours from start to finish.

Ingredients

  • 500g all-purpose flour
  • 7g instant yeast (1 standard packet)
  • 1½ teaspoons fine sea salt
  • 2 tablespoons granulated sugar
  • 350ml warm water (about 40°C / 105°F — warm to the touch, not hot)
  • 80ml extra virgin olive oil, divided (50ml in dough + 30ml for topping)
  • 3-4 fresh rosemary sprigs, leaves stripped
  • 4 cloves garlic, thinly sliced
  • Flaked sea salt (like Maldon) for topping

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Make the dough: In a large bowl, whisk together the flour, instant yeast, salt, and sugar. Add the warm water and 50ml olive oil. Stir with a wooden spoon until a shaggy dough forms. Turn out onto a clean surface and knead for 5 minutes until smooth and slightly tacky (not sticky). Alternatively, use a stand mixer with a dough hook on medium speed for 3 minutes.
  2. First rise: Place the dough in a lightly oiled bowl. Cover with a damp kitchen towel or plastic wrap. Let rise in a warm spot for 1 hour until doubled in size.
  3. Prepare the pan: Generously oil a large baking sheet or 9x13-inch (23x33 cm) baking pan with about 15ml of the remaining olive oil. Make sure the entire surface is coated.
  4. Stretch the dough: Turn the risen dough out onto the oiled pan. Using your fingertips, gently press and stretch the dough to fill the pan. If it springs back, let it relax for 10 minutes, then stretch again. It should be fairly even in thickness, about 2 cm thick.
  5. Dimple and top: Using your fingertips, press deep dimples all over the surface of the dough — push right down to the pan. Drizzle the remaining olive oil generously over the top, letting it pool in the dimples. Press rosemary leaves and thinly sliced garlic into the dough. Sprinkle generously with flaked sea salt.
  6. Second rise: Let the topped dough rest for 20 minutes while you preheat the oven to 220°C (425°F).
  7. Bake: Place in the preheated oven and bake for 20-25 minutes until the top is golden and the bottom is crispy (lift a corner to check). The focaccia should sound hollow when tapped.
  8. Cool and serve: Transfer to a wire rack and let cool for 5 minutes. Slice into squares or rectangles. Serve warm — it's best fresh from the oven, but stays good for 2 days stored in an airtight container.

Tips & Variations

  • Overnight focaccia: For even better flavor, make the dough and let it cold-rise in the fridge overnight (12-18 hours). Bring to room temperature for 1 hour before dimpling, topping, and baking.
  • Don't skimp on olive oil: Focaccia is supposed to be generously oiled. The oil is what creates the crispy, almost fried bottom and edges that make focaccia irresistible.
  • Creative toppings: Try cherry tomatoes and olives, caramelized onions and thyme, or za'atar and sumac for Middle Eastern-inspired focaccia.
  • Make it a sandwich: Slice horizontally and fill with mozzarella, prosciutto, and arugula for the best Italian sandwich you'll ever eat.
