Slow-Cooked Dal Makhani

Prep: 10 min + overnight soak • Cook: 2 hrs • Serves: 6 • 340 kcal per serving

Dal Makhani is the most beloved lentil dish in all of India — and for good reason. Whole black lentils (urad dal) and kidney beans are simmered low and slow until they break down into a thick, creamy, almost meaty texture, then enriched with butter, cream, and a smoky tomato tadka. This recipe takes time, but it rewards you with a dish that's so deeply flavored, you'll wonder why you ever ordered takeout. The secret is patience: the longer you simmer, the better it gets.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup whole black lentils (urad dal), soaked overnight (8+ hours)
  • ¼ cup kidney beans (rajma), soaked overnight
  • 3 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 1 tablespoon neutral oil
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 2 medium tomatoes, pureed
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 teaspoon Kashmiri red chili powder
  • ½ teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • ½ cup heavy cream
  • 1 teaspoon kasuri methi (dried fenugreek leaves)
  • Salt to taste
  • Water as needed (about 4-5 cups)

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Cook the lentils: Drain the soaked lentils and kidney beans. Place in a pressure cooker with 4 cups of water, salt, and turmeric. Pressure cook for 15-20 whistles on medium heat (about 30-40 minutes). Alternatively, simmer in a heavy pot for 1.5-2 hours until completely soft and mushy. The lentils should be falling apart.
  2. Prepare the tadka (tempering): In a separate heavy-bottomed pan, heat butter and oil over medium heat. Add cumin seeds and let them splutter (about 30 seconds). Add the finely chopped onion and cook, stirring frequently, until deep golden brown — this takes a full 10-12 minutes. Don't rush this step; the caramelized onions are crucial for flavor.
  3. Add aromatics and tomato: Add ginger-garlic paste to the golden onions, cook for 1 minute. Add pureed tomatoes and red chili powder. Cook on medium heat, stirring occasionally, until the oil separates from the masala — about 8-10 minutes.
  4. Combine and slow-simmer: Pour the tadka into the pot of cooked lentils. Add ½ cup of water and stir everything together well. Bring to a gentle simmer on the lowest heat possible. Cook for 30 minutes, stirring every 10 minutes to prevent sticking. The dal will thicken and the flavors will meld together beautifully.
  5. Finish with cream and fenugreek: Stir in the heavy cream and garam masala. Crush the kasuri methi between your palms and sprinkle into the dal. Simmer for 5 more minutes. Taste and adjust salt.
  6. Serve: Transfer to a serving bowl. Top with a generous pat of butter that melts into the dal. Serve hot alongside steamed basmati rice or fresh tandoori naan.

Tips & Variations

  • Overnight soak is non-negotiable: Whole urad dal needs at least 8 hours of soaking. Without it, the lentils won't cook to the right creamy consistency.
  • Low and slow wins: The longer you simmer after combining the tadka and lentils, the creamier and more flavorful the dal becomes. Some restaurants simmer for 6-8 hours.
  • Vegan version: Replace butter with vegan butter and cream with cashew cream. The flavor profile changes slightly but remains excellent.
  • Smoky flavor hack: Place a small piece of charcoal (food-grade) on foil inside the pot, drizzle with ghee, and cover for 2 minutes. This gives an authentic tandoori smokiness called dhungar.
